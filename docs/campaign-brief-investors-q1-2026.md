## 2. Target Audience

**Primary Segment: Impact investors and family offices actively allocating to climate infrastructure**

These are funds and private allocators (ticket sizes $250K–$5M) who have carbon or climate as a mandate but are skeptical of low-integrity credit supply. They've seen the Verra/REDD+ scandals, they know episodic MRV is gameable, and they're searching for a more defensible asset.

**Pain points:**
- Carbon credit greenwashing risk is reputational and financial (credits revoked, prices collapse)
- Existing MRV is episodic, manual, and opaque — they can't audit what they bought
- Tokenized carbon has been vaporware — promises without provenance
- No way to verify additionality or permanence post-purchase without trusting a third party

**Motivations:**
- Need defensible, auditable assets they can show LPs and compliance teams
- Want early positioning in infrastructure that will underpin a growing regulated market
- Attracted to protocols with live pilots, real sensor data, and registry integration (Verra)

**Where they spend time:**
- LinkedIn (primary professional network for this cohort)
- Substack newsletters (Carbon Copy, Heatmap, Canary Media, ClimateBase)
- X/Twitter (climate tech and crypto-native capital allocators)
- Conferences: Carbon Unbound, Terraforma, Regenerate, GreenBiz
- Syndicates: Climate Capital, MCJ Collective, Climactic

**Buying stage:** Awareness → Consideration (most don't know Mālama exists yet)

## 3. Key Messages

**Core Message:**
> Carbon integrity is a provenance problem. Mālama solves it at the data layer — before a credit is ever issued.

**Supporting Messages:**

1. **"AI-validated, not self-reported."**
   Mālama's XGBoost confidence engine scores every sensor reading before anything hits the chain. Credits only mint when confidence > 80%. Proof point: SHAP explainability output gives auditors a field-level breakdown of why a reading was accepted or rejected.

2. **"Tamper-evident by construction."**
   Every sensor has a cryptographic DID. Every batch is Merkle-rooted to IPFS and anchored on four chains (Cardano, BASE, HEDERA, CELO). There's no version of this data that can be silently changed. Proof point: Full chain-of-custody from field reading to LCO₂ mint is publicly verifiable by any third party.

3. **"Registry-native, not registry-replacing."**
   Mālama doesn't ask investors to trust a new standard. It submits Proof-of-Journey bundles directly to Verra. The protocol augments existing infrastructure rather than betting on new market acceptance. Proof point: Verra API integration live; Idaho biochar pilot onboarding.

4. **"Programmable carbon, without the hype."**
   LCO₂/VCO₂ tokens are confidence-gated, meaning token supply is mechanically tied to verified sequestration. This is not a synthetic instrument — it's a settlement layer for a real physical process. Proof point: Multi-chain settlement contracts deployed on testnet; mainnet target Q3 2026.

**Channel tone variations:**
- LinkedIn: Technical but accessible. Lead with the integrity problem, follow with the architecture.
- X: Sharper, more native to crypto/climate discourse. Shorter threads, more provocative framing.
- Direct outreach: Warm, specific. Reference the fund's mandate and connect to Mālama's approach.

## 4. Channel Strategy

### LinkedIn (Primary — High Effort)
LinkedIn is the core channel for this sprint. Impact investors, family office principals, and climate fund managers are active here and respond to thoughtful technical content from founders.

**Content format:** Long-form posts (500–900 words), carousel posts (5–8 slides), comment engagement on carbon/climate threads from relevant accounts (Carbon Tracker, Pachama, South Pole, Sylvera).

**Why it fits:** Decision-makers in this space actively scan founder content before taking a call. A 4-week posting cadence builds social proof before direct outreach begins in Week 3.

**Effort:** High (3 posts/week)
**Budget:** $0

### X / Twitter (Secondary — Medium Effort)
A smaller but crypto-native and climate-native audience that overlaps with early-stage investors in tokenized infrastructure. Good for amplifying LinkedIn content and participating in dMRV/carbon market conversations.

**Content format:** Technical threads (5–8 tweets), reposts with commentary, quote-tweets engaging Verra/Gold Standard/carbon market discourse.

**Effort:** Medium (2–3 tweets/week + engagement)
**Budget:** $0

### Direct Outreach via LinkedIn DM + Email (Primary — High Effort)
The highest-leverage lead gen channel at zero budget. A warm, specific, 3-sentence outreach tied to a piece of content (whitepaper or demo video) generates a meaningfully higher response rate than cold boilerplate.

**Targets:** Climate Capital network, MCJ Collective investors, family office principals at allocators with climate mandates, carbon market specialists at larger funds (TPG Rise, Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Prelude Ventures).

**Effort:** High (5–10 personalized outreaches/day in Weeks 3–4)
**Budget:** $0 (LinkedIn Premium already justified at this stage; ~$40/mo if not subscribed)

### Substack / Newsletter Guest Placement (Earned — Low-to-Medium Effort)
A single placement in a high-circulation climate tech newsletter (Carbon Copy, Heatmap, The Defiant for crypto-native readers) can drive 50–200 qualified opens in one day. This takes relationship investment, not money.

**Format:** 500-word contributed piece or founder Q&A.
**Target publications:** Carbon Copy (Adam Pelosi), Heatmap News, Canary Media, The Defiant.
**Effort:** Medium (1–2 pitches in Week 1–2, content ready by Week 2)
**Budget:** $0

### Technical Documentation / Whitepaper (Asset — One-Time Effort)
Not a channel, but the foundational asset that makes all other channels credible. A 4–6 page technical brief covering the Mālama architecture, AI validation methodology, and chain-of-custody model gives investors something to share internally with their technical advisors.

**Effort:** High (produce in Week 1, used across all channels)
**Budget:** $0

## 9. Risks and Mitigations

**Risk 1 — Pilot not live enough to be compelling**
If the Idaho biochar pilot is still in early onboarding by Week 3, content about "real sensors" may feel thin. *Mitigation:* Lead with testnet proof (Merkle roots on-chain are verifiable right now) and frame the pilot as "in progress — here's what we're seeing." Authentic progress beats overstated claims. Do not fabricate metrics.

**Risk 2 — Investor audience is too broad / low response rate**
Generic outreach to "climate investors" will get <5% response. *Mitigation:* Tier the outreach list. Tier 1 (5–10 people) = warm intros from your existing network; Tier 2 (40 people) = targeted cold with personalized first line referencing their specific fund mandate or recent investment; Tier 3 = LinkedIn DM to engaged commenters on your posts. Do not send Tier 3 until you have LinkedIn social proof from Weeks 1–2.

**Risk 3 — Content production bottleneck in Week 1**
The whitepaper and demo video are both required before outreach can credibly launch. If Week 1 production slips, the whole sprint compresses. *Mitigation:* Start the whitepaper draft immediately — pull directly from `docs/agent_process.md` and `docs/prompts.md` architecture documentation. The demo video can be a rough Loom screen-share; polish is secondary to existence.
